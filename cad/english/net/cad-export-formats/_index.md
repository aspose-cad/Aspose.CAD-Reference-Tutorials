## Why export CAD to PNG or JPEG matters

* **Cross‑platform visibility** – PNG and JPEG are universally supported, making it easy to share designs with stakeholders who don’t have CAD software.  
* **Performance** – Raster images load faster in web browsers and mobile apps compared to vector CAD files.  
* **Simplified printing** – High‑resolution PNGs are ideal for large‑format prints, while JPEGs balance quality and file size for quick distribution.

## Common use cases for converting DWG to PDF

* **Construction documentation** – Provide contractors with PDF blueprints that can be printed on‑site.  
* **Legal compliance** – Submit PDFs to permitting authorities that require non‑editable files.  
* **Version control** – Store PDFs in document management systems that lack native DWG support.

## How to export CAD to JPEG efficiently

When generating JPEGs, consider the trade‑off between compression and image clarity. Aspose.CAD lets you set the JPEG quality level, ensuring you get a crisp preview without unnecessary file bloat.

## CAD layout to image – best practices

* Export at **300 dpi** or higher for print‑ready images.  
* Use **transparent backgrounds** (PNG) when you need to overlay the drawing on other graphics.  
* Preserve original **layer visibility** by configuring export options before rendering.
